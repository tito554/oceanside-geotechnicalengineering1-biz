Oceanside's coastal bluffs near the harbor contrast sharply with the inland clay flats around the San Luis Rey River valley. While the beachside lots sit on well-drained dune sands, properties further east, near the 78 corridor, encounter expansive clay layers that change volume with every dry spell and winter rain. That contrast makes unsaturated soil analysis essential for predicting how those clays will behave when moisture content drops below saturation. The soil profile in Oceanside rarely stays uniform across a single lot, so understanding suction and partial saturation helps avoid differential heave. Before any foundation design, the team combines this analysis with a granulometry study to map particle distribution across depth.

In Oceanside's clay flats, a 30 percent drop in saturation can double the shear strength — until the first winter rain reverses it.

Methodology and scope

ASTM D5298-16 governs the filter paper method used to measure matric suction in Oceanside's unsaturated soils, a standard particularly relevant here because the area's Mediterranean climate creates prolonged dry periods followed by intense winter storms. The laboratory measures soil-water characteristic curves (SWCC) using a pressure plate extractor up to 1,500 kPa, covering the full range of suction expected in the region's clayey silts and lean clays. Key parameters include:
  • Matric suction (kPa) at field capacity and wilting point
  • Volumetric water content versus suction curves
  • Hydraulic conductivity under unsaturated conditions
  • Shear strength parameters as a function of degree of saturation
These values feed directly into slope stability and bearing capacity calculations for Oceanside's hillside developments.

Local considerations

A three-story apartment complex near Oceanside Boulevard and College Avenue settled unevenly after two consecutive dry years. The geotechnical report assumed fully saturated conditions for the bearing stratum, but the clay had desiccated to a depth of 4 meters. When winter rains returned, the soil swelled, lifting the slab edge. The cost of retrofitting piers and mudjacking exceeded the original foundation work. Unsaturated soil analysis would have captured the suction-driven strength loss and prevented the under-design. That lesson now drives the lab's standard protocol for any Oceanside site underlain by plastic clays.

Associated technical services

01

Soil-water characteristic curve (SWCC) testing

Full SWCC determination from saturation to oven-dry condition using pressure plate and dewpoint hygrometer. Covers the 0 to 300 MPa suction range for Oceanside's clayey silts and lean clays.

02

Unsaturated shear strength testing

Triaxial and direct shear tests under controlled matric suction using axis translation technique. Provides c' and phi' parameters for partially saturated conditions, critical for Oceanside's hillside cuts and fills.
